On the left is a chart of the frequency of UFO sightings by country. The data spans from 1906 to 2014.

A singular bar immediately stands out. The frequency of sightings in the US is many times greater than the number of sightings of all other countries in the dataset. (Hovering over each bar shows exactly how many such sightings there has been)

What could be the reason for this? Perhaps the biggest influencer is culture. Movies, TV shows, books, and other media have been portraying aliens since the early 20th century. Especially the 1953 film 'The War of the Worlds', as well as the 1898 novel that it was inspired by, contributed greatly to beginnning American's fascination with UFOs and extraterrestrials.

When such a history exists with this unique interest in UFOs, it becomes easier to attribute unknown phenomena to UFO sightings. In other countries where the connection with UFOs is not as deep, it may be that the people of that country attribute unknown phenomena with things other than UFOs. In the next scene, we will explore another way this groupthink may be affecting people's perceptions of UFOs.
